Intro

Hi, my name is Edward Blair. I am a Data Analytics Engineer at Allstate Northern Ireland.

This is my personal portfolio website, which documents my journey in computing. It is composed using HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript elements. My site is decentralised, built on the Ethereum Blockchain and deployed using Fleek. This differs from the majority of portfolio sites in the following ways:

  • Decentralised Hosting: Fleek uses IPFS (InterPlanetary File System), a peer-to-peer (P2P) file system allowing for files to be distributed and stored across multiple nodes. This makes the site resilient to single points of failure and censorship.
  • ENS (Ethereum Name Service): The site is associated with an ENS name, a decentralised domain name system, allowing for a human-readable name to be assigned to a website. This allows for users to access content without relying on traditional, centralised domain registrars.

  1. Year 1
    September 2018

    I was always set on attending Queen's University Belfast, but was unsure about where my true interests lay. At A-level, I discovered my passion and flare for computing, achieving 2nd in Northern Ireland in CCEA Digital Technology. Thus, I ended up pursuing MEng Computer Science at QUB.

  2. Placement Year: Allstate Northern Ireland
    August 2020 - August 2021

    University modules such as Reasoning for Problem Solving, Databases, and Information Modelling sparked my interest in the sphere of Big Data. I wished to apply the knowledge and skills from this these modules in a practical setting. For this reason, I undertook my Year of Professional Experience (YoPE) as a Data Analytics Engineer with Allstate Northern Ireland.

    During my time here, I worked on the development, deployment and testing of three key pipelines, gaining invaluable hands-on experience with industry standard and bespoke tools and technologies used in the fields of data analytics and data science.

  3. Year 3
    September 2021

    I returned to QUB for the penultimate year of study. The modules which interested me most were Malware Analysis and Network Security, which deepened my appreciation of the dynamic and ever-evolving landscape of cybersecurity. These modules allowed us to perform advanced static and dynamic analysis of malware, to gain an understanding of how malicious software behaves.

  4. Summer Internship
    June 2022 - August 2022

    I returned to Allstate NI for a 3-month Summer internship, at the end of which I was delighted to receive the offer of a permanent role.

  5. Final Year
    September 2022 - July 2023

    The main focus in Master's year was upon the Final Year Project. My project was entitled "Preprocessing Techniques for The Model Counting Problem", which applied principles from algorithms, complexity and satisfiability. This problem, also known as #SAT, involves counting the number of distinct satisfying assignments to variables for a given instance of the satisfiability problem (SAT). Myself and my project supervisor explored three preprocessing techniques in detail, and proposed a novel metric to quantify how well the variables of a boolean formula are ordered.

    In July 2023, I graduated with First Class Honours in MEng Computer Science Including Professional Experience.

  6. Allstate Northern Ireland
    August 2023

    In August 2023, I began my graduate role as a Data Analytics Engineer with Allstate Northern Ireland.
